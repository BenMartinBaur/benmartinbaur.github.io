# Squad Operating Charter

Status: Approved v1.0
Effective date: 2026-06-29
Owner: Toto (Chief of Staff Lead)
Scope: Blog, brand strategy, and content operations for this repository
Approved on: 2026-06-29

## 1) Mission

Build a trusted, high-leverage publishing system that helps people make better decisions and execute faster.

## 2) Core Principles

1. Unlock potential.
2. Always inclusive and respectful.
3. Share what we have learned.
4. Be a 10x engineer.
5. Insight on the highest standards

## 3) Policy Hierarchy

When guidance conflicts, apply this precedence:

1. Core Principles (this charter, Section 2)
2. Operating Charter clauses (this document)
3. Quality standards and checklists
4. Backlog and sprint commitments
5. Local task decisions

## 4) Audience Priority Clause

Every content item must declare:

1. One primary audience.
2. One audience job-to-be-done.
3. One intended decision or action outcome.

Allowed audiences:

1. Builders (hands-on practitioners)
2. Strategy leaders (decision-makers)
3. Career growth readers (leadership and personal effectiveness)

## 5) Evidence and Confidence Clause

For all non-opinion claims:

1. Add evidence type: field pattern, cited source, or hypothesis.
2. Add confidence level: high, medium, or low.
3. Add a verification timestamp for time-sensitive facts.

No strong claim ships without evidence annotation.

## 6) Inclusion, Respect, and Accessibility Clause

Before publish, all content must pass:

1. Plain-language check (define acronyms and reduce jargon density).
2. Inclusive framing check (no exclusionary assumptions by role, region, or resources).
3. Accessibility check (alt text, heading structure, descriptive links, readable structure).

## 7) AI Assistance Transparency Clause

If AI support is used in creation, disclose scope in internal notes and keep human accountability for final decisions.

Minimum disclosure fields:

1. Where AI helped (brainstorming, structure, editing, or coding support).
2. What was human-verified.
3. Final accountable reviewer.

## 8) Lifecycle and Freshness Clause

All content moves through mandatory lifecycle states:

1. Idea
2. Draft
3. Review
4. Publish
5. Refresh or Retire

SLA guardrails:

1. Idea older than 30 days: re-score or archive.
2. Draft older than 14 days: escalate for unblock or de-scope.
3. Published piece older than 180 days in high-change topics: revalidate.

## 9) Stage Gates and Definition of Done

A content item is done only when all gates pass.

Gate 1: Audience and objective gate

1. Primary audience and intended outcome are explicit.
2. Topic maps to one brand pillar.

Gate 2: Technical and factual gate

1. Claims are evidence-tagged.
2. References and links are valid.

Gate 3: Values and inclusion gate

1. Inclusive and respectful language checks pass.
2. Accessibility baseline passes.

Gate 4: Brand and voice gate

1. Tone aligns to practical, clear, and credible voice.
2. Actionability is explicit.

Gate 5: Publish and distribution gate

1. Distribution plan is attached.
2. Success metrics and review date are assigned.

## 10) Distribution and Learning Clause

Publishing is not complete at release.

Each published piece must include:

1. Channel distribution sequence.
2. At least one derivative asset.
3. A 14-day learning review with actions.

## 11) Sustainable Pace Clause

High standards do not require unsustainable output.

Rules:

1. Default to sustainable cadence over hero bursts.
2. Use minimum viable publish format when capacity drops.
3. Quality floor is mandatory even under time pressure.

## 12) Incident, Correction, and Rollback Clause

Severity model:

1. P1: trust or safety critical
2. P2: high-impact factual or technical issue
3. P3: minor issue

Response expectations:

1. P1 acknowledged same day and corrected or rolled back immediately.
2. P2 corrected within 48 hours.
3. P3 corrected in next scheduled content maintenance cycle.

Corrections must be dated and visible in update notes.

## 13) Governance Model

Cadence:

1. Weekly operations review (scoreboard and blockers)
2. Biweekly process health review (flow and quality)
3. Monthly charter calibration (clauses, targets, and thresholds)

Decision accountability:

1. One accountable owner per major decision.
2. Major decisions logged in squad decisions inbox.

## 14) Escalation Rules

Escalate when:

1. Blocker age exceeds 48 hours.
2. Item breaches SLA by 20 percent or more.
3. P1 or P2 incident is detected.

Escalation path:

1. Role owner
2. Toto
3. Team-wide prioritization reset

## 15) Weekly Scoreboard Requirement

The weekly scoreboard is mandatory and is the operating source of truth.

Required metrics:

1. Backlog readiness rate
2. WIP ratio
3. Throughput
4. Cycle time p50 and p85
5. Blocker burden
6. Blocker aging
7. SLA compliance
8. Review latency
9. Rework rate
10. Commitment reliability
11. Learning capture completion
12. Audience outcome signal

Template location: .squad/weekly-scoreboard-template.md

## 16) Non-Functional Requirements

1. Reliability: predictable weekly publish flow.
2. Quality: objective gate pass criteria always enforced.
3. Observability: scoreboard updated weekly without fail.
4. Resilience: fallback approver path exists for blocked gate stages.
5. Traceability: decision rationale is recorded for high-impact changes.

## 17) Amendment Rule

This charter changes only through monthly calibration or incident-driven emergency update with logged rationale.
