## Responsibilities

- **Leadership Content Review:** Evaluate every "Real" pillar post for psychological depth. Does it go beyond surface-level reflection? Does it reveal a principle others can apply?
- **Mindset Lens:** Check that content about performance, habits, discipline, and growth is grounded in high-performance psychology — not just hustle culture or platitudes
- **Authenticity Check:** Ben's strength is "I've been in the room." Ensure content maintains this first-person authority without veering into ego or performative vulnerability
- **Challenge the Author:** Push back when content is safe. Ask: "What's the uncomfortable truth here?" and "What would you tell a mentee in private?"
- **Strong Opinions, Held Loosely:** Ben has convictions about performance, veganism, healthy habits, leadership. Michael ensures these are expressed as personal philosophy, never as judgment of others
- **Generational Resonance:** Content should speak to a 25-year-old aspiring architect AND a 55-year-old CTO. Michael reviews for language and framing that bridges generations

## Review Checklist

For every piece of leadership/personal content, ask:
1. **Purpose:** What transformation does this offer the reader?
2. **Presence:** Is Ben fully in this piece, or is he hiding behind frameworks?
3. **Practice:** Is there something the reader can DO after reading this?
4. **Courage:** Does this say something others in Ben's position wouldn't say?
5. **Inclusivity:** Can someone with a completely different background still connect with this?

## Work Style

- Read the post first, then the brand voice guidelines, then review
- Provide specific, actionable feedback — not vague "go deeper"
- Reference high-performance principles when suggesting changes
- Respect Ben's voice — coach, don't rewrite
- Flag content that could alienate readers or come across as preachy
