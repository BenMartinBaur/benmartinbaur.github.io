## Unfair Advantage

Most tech bloggers are *either* deep engineers *or* strategy consultants. Ben is **both** — and sits in Microsoft's CTO office with direct customer access. Every piece of content should reinforce: *"I've been in the room, I've built the thing, and here's what actually works."*

## Content Pillars (Categories)

| Category | Name | Description | Content Types |
|----------|------|-------------|---------------|
| 🏗️ `build` | Hands-on Engineering | Deep technical content with real tools and architectures | PowerShell tools, Azure walkthroughs, architecture patterns, IaC templates |
| 🗺️ `strategy` | Frameworks & Thinking | Strategic frameworks, comparisons, and decision tools | CAF/AI frameworks, BXT, CoE models, cheatsheets, decision trees |
| 🎤 `real` | Honest Reflections | Personal voice, lessons learned, leadership philosophy | Career stories, reflections, "what I learned," values posts |

**Rule:** Every post belongs to exactly one pillar. Every month should have content from at least 2 of 3.

## Voice & Tone

| Attribute | Do | Don't |
|-----------|-----|-------|
| **Authority** | Speak from experience: "In my customer sessions…" | Lecture: "You should always…" |
| **Honesty** | Name uncomfortable truths: "Most AI projects fail because…" | Hedge: "It might be worth considering…" |
| **Practical** | Always include a takeaway, tool, or framework | Write theory-only with no action |
| **Conversational** | Write like explaining to a smart colleague | Use academic or corporate-speak |
| **Inclusive** | "Here's what I've learned" | "Here's what you're doing wrong" |

**Signature phrases:**
- *"Born on premise — living in the cloud"*
- *"Think in systems"*
- *"Hit refresh"*
- *"If you can't explain it to a CTO and a Cloud Engineer, you don't understand it"*
