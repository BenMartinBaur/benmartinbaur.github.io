Promotion Philosophy --> STU Fundamentals

Company Values:
    1. Respect
    2. Integrity
    3. Accountability

Leadership Principles:
    • Create Clarity with every action you take.
    • Generating Energy (maintaining optimism)
    • Delivering Success (ruthless priorities)
    1. Leadership
        1. Make your tasks programmatic so it is reproducible and sharable
        2. Share failures with the same energy as your successes
        3. Manage the distance between you and your engineering counterpart
    2. Growth
        1. Work with your manager to identify your strengths and your development areas
        2. Identifying your development areas is your first step toward growth.  Embrace them, as demonstrating your ability to accept feedback and grow is the key to moving your career forward.
    3. Collaboration
        1. Nothing great was ever achieved single handedly
        2. As the feature owner, you get credit for the development of your feature area and your ability to lead it.
        3. As you move into more senior roles, you will do less individual contribution.  This transition can be difficult, begin exercising these muscles early
    4. Meetings
        1. One meeting owner who keep the meeting on track (they should be on the only person managing a meetings pace)
        2. Before meetings start send the deck / agenda / docs
        3. Before meeting starts create a "parking lot" on the whiteboard
            1. Parking lot is where any deeper discussions would be captured
            2. An owner needs to be assigned to any item
            3. All parties interested in the follow-up should be identified (they can write their own names down)
            4. Meeting notes should reflect the parking lot items, owner and interested parties
            5. Parking lot item owner is responsible for sending follow-ups (explanation via email, meeting invite, etc)
        4. Before meeting starts elect a notetaker
            1. Responsible for capturing follow-ups
            2. Responsible sending meeting notes to attendees
    5. Functional Specs
        1. Your primary canvas is your functional specs
        2. Circulate your spec with your dev/DI counterparts first (or better yet work directly with them as you create it)
        3. Schedule spec reviews and invite required and optional attendees
        4. Send spec 48 hours prior from sharepoint with change tracking turned on and request comments in the spec.
        5. Review the comments 24 hours prior to spec review and determine if you are prepared to hold spec review as planned
        6. Meet with your dev/DI counterparts just prior to spec review and ensure you are aligned!
        7. Encourage your dev/DI counterparts to respond to questions / feedback during the review.  You are leading the meeting but it doesn't mean you should be doing all the talking.
