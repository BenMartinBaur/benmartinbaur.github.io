80% of AI projects fail.

Not because the models are wrong. Not because the data science team isn't talented. Not because the technology isn't ready.

They fail because of **leadership problems disguised as technical ones.**

RAND Corporation researchers interviewed 65 experienced data scientists and engineers to find out why. They uncovered 5 root causes — and every single one is preventable:

1️⃣ **Miscommunication** — Teams don't agree on what problem they're solving
2️⃣ **Data gaps** — Organisations start building before the data is ready
3️⃣ **Shiny tech syndrome** — Chasing the latest model instead of solving real problems
4️⃣ **Infrastructure neglect** — Brilliant prototypes with no path to production
5️⃣ **Overreach** — Applying AI to problems it can't (yet) solve

Notice a pattern? None of these are algorithm problems. They're alignment problems.

In my work at Microsoft's Office of the CTO, I see this play out every week. The enterprises that succeed with AI aren't the ones with the best models — they're the ones that start with a clearly defined problem, invest in data and infrastructure *before* model development, and have the intellectual honesty to say "this isn't an AI problem" when it isn't.

I wrote a detailed breakdown of the research, the patterns I see in the field, and a practical fix for each root cause.
