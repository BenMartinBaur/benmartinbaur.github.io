You can't out-hustle biology.

I used to treat nourishment as optional.

Something to squeeze in after the strategy work was done.

But elite athletes and top executives taught me a different truth:
performance is built on foundations, not adrenaline.

One insight that stayed with me from Dr. Michael Gervais and Finding Mastery:
when the brain is expected to make high-stakes decisions all day, fuel is not a luxury.
It is part of the job.

For me, Intentional Nourishment is a quiet leadership decision:
The brain doing all that strategic work deserves fuel.

Here is what shifted when I took this seriously:

→ Fewer reactive decisions in the late afternoon
→ Better emotional steadiness under pressure
→ More clarity in conversations that actually matter

No dramatic hacks.
Just consistent nourishment, before depletion hits.

Today’s Micro Challenge:
Pick one meeting block today and fuel before it starts.
A real meal.
Water.
A 5-minute pause to check in with your body.

Then notice: Did your thinking feel sharper?

If you accept the challenge, react with a 🥳 and share what changed.
