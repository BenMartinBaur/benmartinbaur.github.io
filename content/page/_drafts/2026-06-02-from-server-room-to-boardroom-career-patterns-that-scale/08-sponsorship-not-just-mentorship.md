## Core Promise
Readers will understand the mechanics of sponsorship and take concrete action to improve advocacy around their work.

## Key Points To Cover
- Mentorship gives advice; sponsorship creates access.
- Why many high performers stall without sponsor support.
- How to become "sponsor-worthy" through trust and consistency.
- How to sponsor others before formal leadership title.

## Story Anchor Prompts
- A moment where someone advocated for your work and changed trajectory.
- A moment where lack of sponsorship was the hidden blocker.

## Anti-Pattern Section
"Networking theater": collecting contacts without creating mutual value.

## Framework Block
Sponsor Map:
- Who knows your work?
- Who trusts your execution?
- Who can advocate in decision forums?
- What proof points support that advocacy?

## 15-Minute Reader Exercise
Ask readers to build a sponsor map with three names and one concrete value action for each relationship.

## CTA
"Who is one person you can help this month without expecting anything back?"

## Draft Checklist
- Keep ethics explicit
- Avoid manipulative framing
- Add practical scripts for outreach
- Include inclusion caveat for uneven access to sponsorship
