# How AI Became My Personal Chief of Staff Team

I've spent years helping enterprises adopt AI. I've run workshops, scored use cases with the BXT Framework, and told countless customers: _"Start with a real problem, not a technology."_

So I decided to take my own advice.

The problem was simple: I'm a Technical Architect at Microsoft's Innovation Hub and Office of the CTO. I write about AI strategy, cloud architecture, and leadership. I have opinions, frameworks, and field experience — but I'm one person. I don't have a brand strategist. I don't have a social media manager. I don't have a communications team reviewing my content before it goes live.

What if AI could be all of those things?

Not as a gimmick. Not as a "look what ChatGPT can do" demo. But as a genuine operating model — an AI-powered Chief of Staff team that manages my personal brand, reviews my content, and helps me scale what I can't do alone.

This post documents that experiment. Every finding below is real. Every recommendation came from AI auditing my actual blog. And everything that follows is the joint roadmap we're executing together.

---

## The Setup

I gave AI a clear brief:

> _"You are my Chief of Staff team — a social media brand manager, strategy and communications manager, and content writer. Your first task: review my entire blog and create a brand strategy for me."_

The AI reviewed:
- Every blog post (10 posts across technical, strategic, and personal content)
- My About page and career history
- My LinkedIn profile (including the 4-year content gap)
- All 27 tags and the category structure
- The Hugo site configuration
- My awards, public presence, and community contributions

What it returned wasn't generic advice. It was a surgical audit with findings I hadn't seen myself — despite staring at my own blog for weeks.

## The Audit: What AI Found

### What's Working

| Strength | Why It Matters |
|----------|---------------|
| **Rare positioning** | Deep technical credibility *and* boardroom strategy thinking — most people are one or the other |
| **Authentic voice** | "I sit in these rooms every week" — writing from lived experience, not theory |
| **High-utility content** | Cheatsheets, scorecards, comparison tables — content people bookmark and share |
| **Framework-oriented** | CAF vs AI, BXT, ORDER — thinking in systems and making them accessible |
| **Strong About page** | Clear career arc, impressive credentials, human touches |

### What's Broken

This is where it got uncomfortable. AI found issues I'd been blind to:

| Issue | Detail | Status |
|-------|--------|--------|
| **Broken post** | "First 30 Days" photo gallery was leaking raw Hugo markdown syntax — `content/gallery/my-first-gallery/index.md` was visible to every visitor | ✅ Fixed |
| **Typos in published content** | "plat-based" instead of "plant-based" (3x), "frist" instead of "first", "adn" instead of "and" in the About page | ✅ Fixed |
| **No content pillars** | Mix of Azure networking, AI strategy, consulting methodology, and vegan lifestyle — no structure | ✅ Done |
| **Broken categories** | Only 1 useful category — not useful for navigation or SEO | ✅ Done |
| **27 tags, no hierarchy** | Tags like "Hugo", "Markdown", "Shortcodes" alongside "AI" and "Strategy" — noise | ✅ Done |
| **No CTA anywhere** | No newsletter, no follow prompt, no engagement loop on any post | 📋 Planned |
| **LinkedIn gap** | 8K followers but last article was March 2022 — a 4-year content gap | 📋 Planned |
| **No visual brand identity** | No logo, no consistent color palette, no branded imagery across posts | ✅ Done → Coral accent, three-pillar image system |
| **Generic blog name** | "The Architect" is good but undifferentiated — hundreds of blogs share this name | ✅ Done → **B3N.B4UR_** |

The broken post and typos were the worst. I'd been sharing links to a blog where raw markdown was visible to anyone who scrolled to page 2. AI caught it in minutes. I'd missed it for weeks.

## The Brand Strategy

### Positioning

AI distilled my positioning into one statement:

> _"A Technical Architect at Microsoft's Office of the CTO who bridges the gap between boardroom strategy and hands-on engineering — helping enterprises turn AI ambition into scalable architecture."_

One-liner for social bios:

> _Technical Architect @ Microsoft CTO Office · Turning AI strategy into architecture that ships · Pinnacle Award winner · 🏄‍♂️ 🎧 🌱_

### The Three Rooms — Content Pillars

AI proposed organising all content into three "rooms" — which now map directly to the blog's category structure:

| Category | Name | What Goes Here |
|----------|------|---------------|
| 🏗️ `build` | Hands-on Engineering | PowerShell tools, Azure walkthroughs, architecture patterns, IaC templates |
| 🗺️ `strategy` | Frameworks & Thinking | CAF/AI comparisons, BXT, CoE models, cheatsheets, decision trees |
| 🎤 `real` | Honest Reflections | Career stories, lessons learned, leadership philosophy, personal values |

**Rule:** Every post belongs to exactly one room. Every month should have content from at least 2 of 3.

### Voice Guidelines

| Attribute | Do | Don't |
|-----------|-----|-------|
| **Authority** | "In my customer sessions…" | "You should always…" |
| **Honesty** | "Most AI projects fail because…" | "It might be worth considering…" |
| **Practical** | Always include a takeaway or tool | Theory-only posts with no action |
| **Conversational** | Write like explaining to a smart colleague | Academic or corporate-speak |

### Signature Phrases to Own

- *"Born on premise — living in the cloud"*
- *"Think in systems"*
- *"Hit refresh"*
- *"If you can't explain it to a CTO and a Cloud Engineer, you don't understand it"*

## The Roadmap

### Content Cadence

| Channel | Frequency | Content |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| **Blog** | 2x/month | 1 deep post (Build or Strategy Room) + 1 lighter post (Real Room) |
| **LinkedIn** | 3x/week | Post excerpts, hot takes, framework snippets |
| **LinkedIn Articles** | 1x/month | Republish best blog post for LinkedIn's algorithm |
| **GitHub** | Ongoing | Publish tools and scripts alongside blog posts |

### Content Series

| Series | Pillar | Format |
|--------|--------|--------|
| **Framework Fridays** | 🗺️ Strategy | One cheatsheet/framework per month |
| **From the Field** | 🎤 Real | Customer engagement reflections (anonymised) |
| **The Outage Report** | 🏗️ Build | Deep-dive on Azure challenges with scripts/tooling |
| **Boardroom Briefing** | 🗺️ Strategy | C-level topic explained for execs and engineers |

### Visual Identity

| Element | Direction |
|---------|-----------|
| **Primary colour** | `#1E2761` (Midnight Navy) — authority, trust |
| **Accent colour** | `#F96167` (Coral) — warmth, stands out in feeds |
| **Blog tagline** | *"Where Strategy Meets the Stack"* |
| **Featured images** | Consistent branded template across all posts |

## The Meta-Lesson

I tell customers every week: _"AI value doesn't come from more experiments. It comes from aligning strategy, people, data, governance, and execution."_

I didn't need more posts. I needed someone to look at what I already had with fresh eyes, find the broken pieces, and build a system for what comes next.

AI did that in one afternoon.

**If you don't change it, you choose it.** So I changed it.
